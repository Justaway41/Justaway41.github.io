'Kono Subarashii Sekai ni Shukufuku wo!' Gets New Anime Project

The Konosuba Channel from Isekai Museum livestream event announced a new anime project for Natsume Akatsuki's Kono Subarashii Sekai ni Shukufuku wo! (Konosuba: God's Blessing on This Wonderful World!) light novel on Sunday. The event also revealed a key visual (pictured above).

Akatsuki originally penned the adventure comedy light novel, abbreviated as Konosuba, on the Shousetsuka ni Narou website from December 2012 to October 2013. Kadokawa began printing the novel with illustrations by Kurone Mishima through its Sneaker Bunko label the month the web novel ended. The light novel ended with its 17th volume on May 1 last year.

Studio Deen produced the first two television anime with ten episodes each during Winter 2016 and 2017. J.C.Staff produced an anime movie that premiered in Japan in August 2019. The ninth and 12th volumes also included two original video anime.

Crunchyroll and Discotek Media licensed both anime seasons for streaming and home video releases, respectively.

'Beastars' Anime Series Receives Sequel

Toho opened an official website for a sequel of the anime series adapting Paru Itagaki's Beastars manga on Wednesday. Studio Orange is returning from the previous seasons to produce the sequel.

The first season premiered in Fall 2019 on Fuji TV's +Ultra programming block, followed by a second season in Winter 2021â€”each running for 12 episodes. Netflix began streaming the seasons worldwide last March and July 15, respectively.

Itagaki began drawing the psychological drama manga in Shounen Champion magazine in September 2016 and ended it last October. Akita Shoten published the 22nd and final volume on January 8.

VIZ Media licensed the manga in English in November 2018 and began publishing it the following July. The 13th volume shipped on Tuesday.

'Boku no Hero Academia the Movie: World Heroes' Mission' Announces New Cast
The official website of Boku no Hero Academia the Movie: World Heroes' Mission anime film announced six new cast members on Saturday (pictured above). The third anime film in the franchise will open in theaters in Japan on August 6.

Cast
Beros: Mariya Ise (Sekai Seifuku: Bouryaku no Zvezda)
Sidero: Yuuichirou Umehara (Tenkuu Shinpan)
Serpenters: Junya Enoki (Kanojo mo Kanojo)

Production staff returning for the third film at Bones include Kenji Nagasaki , scriptwriter Yousuke Kuroda , character designer Yoshihiko Umakoshi , and music composer Yuuki Hayashi . Original manga creator Kouhei Horikoshi is serving as the chief supervisor.

'Fruits Basket' Side Story 'Kyouko to Katsuya no Monogatari' Announced for 2022
The 13th and final episode of Fruits Basket: The Final, the third and final season of the anime series, announced a new anime titled Kyouko to Katsuya no Monogatari (The Story of Kyouko and Katsuya) following its broadcast on Tuesday. The anime will debut in 2022.

The side story anime will adapt chapters from volume 16 of the original manga, centering on Kyouko and Katsuyaâ€”parents of the female protagonist Tohru Honda. Miyuki Sawashiro (Noragami) and Yoshimasa Hosoya (Bungou Stray Dogs) will are reprising their roles as Kyouko and Katsuya, respectively.

Fruits Basket adapts Natsuki Takaya's supernatural shoujo manga, which ran in Hana to Yume magazine from 1998 to 2006. Takaya serialized the Fruits Basket: Another sequel manga between September 2015 and December 2018.

The first two seasons aired in 25 episodes in Spring 2019 and 2020. The third and final season began airing on April 6. Crunchyroll and Funimation simulcast all three seasons with subtitles and in English dub, respectively. Funimation also licensed the anime's home video rights and released the first two seasons on home video between November 2019 and March 23.

'Chainsaw Man' TV Anime Unveils Production Staff
The MAPPA Stage 2021: 10th Anniversary event unveiled a teaser promo and the production staff for the television anime adaptation of Tatsuki Fujimoto's Chainsaw Man manga on Sunday.

Staff
Director: Ryuu Nakayama (Jujutsu Kaisen episode director)
Script: Hiroshi Seko (Dorohedoro)
Character Design: Kazutaka Sugiyama (Mushoku Tensei: Isekai Ittara Honki Dasu)
Action Director: Tatsuya Yoshihara (Black Clover director)
Chief Director: Makoto Nakazono (SSSS.Gridman episode director)
Devil Design: Kiyotaka Oshiyama (Devilman: Crybaby)
Art Director: Yuusuke Takeda (Vinland Saga)
Color Design: Naomi Nakano (Sword Art Online: Alicization)
Layout Design: Youhei Miyahara (Bounen no Xamdou director of photography)
Music: Kensuke Ushio (Boogiepop wa Warawanai (2019))
Animation Producer: Keisuke Seshita (Jujutsu Kaisen)

Following the conclusion of his Fire Punch manga in Shounen Jump+ in January 2018, Fujimoto launched the horror action manga in Weekly Shounen Jump in December that year. The manga ended with 97 chapters on December 14 last year. Shueisha published the 11th and final volume on March 4. Chainsaw Man has a cumulative 11 million copies
